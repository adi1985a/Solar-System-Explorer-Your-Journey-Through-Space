What Makes Up Our Universe?

Ordinary Matter

Only 5% of the universe is made of visible matter - stars, planets, galaxies, and everything we can see.

Dark Matter

About 27% is mysterious dark matter, invisible but detectable through its gravitational effects.

Dark Energy

The remaining 68% is dark energy, a force causing the universe's expansion to accelerate.

Fascinating Space Phenomena

Black Holes

Regions of space where gravity is so strong that nothing can escape, not even light.

Supernovas

Explosive deaths of massive stars that can briefly outshine entire galaxies.

Neutron Stars

Incredibly dense stellar remnants with extreme magnetic fields.

Gamma-Ray Bursts

The most energetic explosions known in the universe.
